Engineering Approach
How I
Build
01
System-First

Architecture & interfaces defined before any hardware or code.

02
Modularity

Subsystems independently testable. Cascading failure = design failure.

03
Constraint Awareness

Power, thermal, latency, cost — design inputs, not afterthoughts.

04
Iterative Prototyping

Physical builds exist to surface assumptions, not confirm them.

05
Scalability

Every system designed to scale across fleet, load, and environment.

I design for deployment, not demonstration. Accounting for real-world sensor noise, thermal behavior under sustained load, and failure modes that only emerge in field conditions — not in simulation.

Working at the intersection of mechanical systems, embedded firmware, and autonomous software has given me a cross-layer perspective that is difficult to develop in siloed contexts. The hardware-software boundary is a co-design surface, not a handoff point.

Every subsystem answers two questions: how does it fail gracefully, and how does it scale?
